Predicting Amino Acid Content from Food Labels

Senior Capstone Regression Forests Python flok Health

People with rare metabolic disorders like PKU must carefully track phenylalanine intake, but estimating it for manufactured foods is a slow, manual process. For my senior capstone, in collaboration with flok Health, I designed and implemented a regression forest model that predicts phenylalanine content directly from standard nutrition label data — replacing hand-estimation with a fast, reproducible pipeline that can serve the rare disease community.

Multi-Modal Deep Learning for Energy Demand Forecasting

Deep Learning Transformers CNNs PyTorch

A multi-modal forecasting architecture that fuses image data with tabular time-series to predict energy demand. A shared CNN encoder downsamples each timestep's imagery into compact 32-dimensional embeddings, which flow into a lightweight transformer (4 layers, 4 heads) alongside tabular tokens with zero-masked future demand. The design prioritizes computational efficiency — small embedding dimensions, shared weights, and a compact MLP prediction head — without sacrificing forecast quality.

Reconstructing the Earth from Geodesic Distances Using Classical MDS

MDS Manifold Learning Procrustes Alignment Math 123

Suppose every map on Earth vanished, and all you had was a traveler's notebook of walking distances between thousands of cities. Could you rebuild the world map? With Alex Bronstein, I used classical multidimensional scaling to reconstruct global geography from geodesic distances alone — distances that break MDS's core Euclidean assumption, since they live on a curved sphere. Reconstructing the whole globe at once gave heavy distortion (~800 km average error), but partitioning into continents, countries, and finally 5°×5° grid cells — then stitching the pieces back together with Procrustes alignment — drove error down to just 0.01 km, demonstrating that curvature can be avoided through localization, but never modeled by MDS itself.

Movie Recommendation System

Collaborative Filtering SVD++ Ensemble Methods CS 135

Built a movie recommender on the MovieLens dataset in two stages: first, latent-factor collaborative filtering implemented from scratch with SGD and autograd, exploring how factor count and L2 regularization shape overfitting. Then an open-ended challenge combining SVD++ (implicit feedback) and KNNBaseline (neighborhood similarity) into a weighted ensemble — reaching 0.701 MAE on the class leaderboard, better than either model alone. The learned embeddings even clustered films by franchise and separated beloved originals from their weaker sequels. With Sam Mard.

Classifying Reading-Level Difficulty from Text

NLP BERT Embeddings Logistic Regression CS 135

Can a model tell whether a passage is written for lower or upper reading levels? Starting from a custom bag-of-words pipeline (hand-rolled tokenization, vocabulary construction, and leakage-safe cross-validation), then upgrading to BERT embeddings with PCA and author-grouped K-fold validation to honestly measure generalization to unseen writers. The BERT pipeline improved test AUROC from 0.700 to 0.732, and error analysis showed the model performs best on longer, more complex passages. With Sam Mard.
